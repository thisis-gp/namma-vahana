# ParkPulse — 90-second demo script

Run `streamlit run app/Home.py`. Narrate while clicking. Times are cumulative.

**[0–12s] Landing / the hook**
> "Bengaluru Police logged 298,000 illegal-parking violations in five months — but enforcement is reactive, chasing yesterday's spots. ParkPulse fixes that."
*(Point at the hero numbers: +13 pp uplift, Precision@20 0.85.)*

**[12–25s] Enter as Police → pick a station**
> "An officer signs in and scopes to their station." *(Sign in as Police → choose a station, e.g. Upparpet.)*
*(Top banner shows the proven outcome immediately.)*

**[25–45s] Hotspots tab — the map + a hotspot**
> "Every hotspot is scored by congestion impact — a transparent proxy from the data, not a fake speed number." *(Click the tallest column.)*
> "Each one gets a plain-language brief: road type, peak time, and whether it needs targeted enforcement or an infrastructure fix."

**[45–60s] Deployment & Outcome tab — the money slide**
> "Here's the proof. On days the model never saw, ParkPulse covers 23% of next-day congestion impact versus 10% for reactive patrol — more than double, with the same patrols." *(Drag the what-if slider.)*
> "And it outputs a ready-to-use patrol roster." *(Click download CSV.)*

**[60–72s] Forecast & Events**
> "A next-week forecast per station with confidence bands — and officers can declare a known event, like an IPL match, to surge the forecast live."

**[72–85s] Citizen view**
> "Same engine, second audience: a public risk guide showing where and when fines are likely — in plain language." *(Exit → Enter as Citizen.)*

**[85–90s] Close**
> "Built entirely on the provided data, no external sources, with every caveat stated. Same officers — smarter placement. That's ParkPulse."
